Better Alone? is a project about social anxiety in the presence of an unknown character in a crowded train station. The project explores the anxiety one might feel being in large crowds of people while seeking comfort by alienating themselves. The character must traverse the crowd only to find herself missing the crowded train towards home. She finds comfort as she waits alone for the last train that is often empty. She sleeps through the night and has a nightmare of the people she may encounter the next day. Nightmare becomes reality and the cycle continues. Is it better to be alone in a world that is otherwise full of people?

2019, Dimensions: 1920p x 1080p, Medium: Video

Against the Current is a game about social anxiety. The player must traverse a never ending crowd of people. The player can choose to move forward (against the crowd) while increasing their feelings of nervousness or the player can choose to interact with people to decrease their nervous feelings and increase their confidence.The player moves through the crowd easier/faster if they are confident. If the player is nervous they move slower through the crowd. The player will also engage in a situation that places the player against themself, forcing the player in a standstill thus preventing any progress. It is a person's own judgement and willingness to move against their anxiety in order to make it better. Towards the end, nothing can be completely forgotten thus the player's controls remain the same but move in the opposite direction. The game concludes the same way it began - this time moving with the current.
2019, Dimensions: 1500p x 500p, Medium: HTML Game
Against the Current

DataProtector.jk (just kidding) is a website that exploits a user's disregard of security measures or incapability to recognize an unsafe website. DataProtector.jk uses a common online scam called "phishing" to send emails disguised as an authentic website (e.g. Facebook, Wells Fargo, etc.) to gain a user's login information. DataProtector.jk makes use of a user's onboard microphone to literally and metaphorically collect the user's data then output that data into visible changes similar to how a user might notice a small/large change in their bank account balance.
